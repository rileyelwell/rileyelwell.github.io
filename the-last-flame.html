    • The Last Flame

    ABOUT

    A dying world. A burning spirit. The fragile hope of the last flame.

    2025 (4 Weeks)
    Tech & UX Design
    Group of 20
    Unity, C#, Perforce, Jira

    Theme & Ideation

    Having a large team with diverse ideas made initial design discussions more challenging than normal. To make it as smooth as possible, we used Miro to express mechanics, themes, art-styles, genres, and team goals.

    Given a theme restriction of 'Growth & Fragility', we decided on a narrative-driven game where fire acts as a friend instead of a foe.

    Playing as a mystical fire fox in a frozen wasteland, you must spread your flame along with its light and warmth to surviors. The frozen environment and its creatures can extinguish your flame and represent the fragility theme in our design.

    Diegetic Main Menu

    I believe that a strong first impression is crucial, so I spent a lot of time on the main menu.

    Inspired by Bioshock: Infinite's diegetic UI, I developed a world-space canvas with interactive panels and camera transitions to immerse players in the game's environment before they even started playing.

    Lead Designer Role

    My role was to ensure design task completion and cohesion while communicating with other leads and the product owner. Given the skills of the team, I focused heavily on UI/UX designs, helping out with technical aspects when required.

    Also, with no dedicated QA role, I often found myself playtesting and tackling bugs in various areas of the game.

    Subtitles

    Focusing on accessibility, I did research before diving into the subtitle visuals, learning more about color, fonts, constrast, and spacing.

    Emphasizing clarity and minimalism in the dark environment, I chose a readable white font, synced it with audio via scriptable objects, and displayed speaker names in distinct colors on separate lines.

    Interaction Prompts & Checkpoints

    Minimal, diegetic UI prompts in the dark environment presented a challenge, especially after feedback from playtesters.

    Collaborating with a programmer and artist, we implemented a system where bright, prioritized icons appear within a radius and rotate towards the camera for constant visibility.

    When the player enters a checkpoint (ruin area) I shift to a static camera to indicate its importance as well as showing the items needed to activate it in a coherent way.

    Player Settings & Controller Support

    Using the Newtonsoft Json package in Unity I saved player settings in a JSON file which I could reference and later load in.

    I discovered the difficulties of getting multiple input devices to work properly, especially in user interfaces and those that are diegetic.

    To solve this, I made a script to toggle between button selections in specific orders, after setting them up in the editor hierarchy.

    Artifact Collectables & Codex

    We needed a codex for the player to access important information, and after many iterations I settled on a diegetic camera-based system to be less disruptive.

    Inspired by our artist's beautiful starry skybox, I implemented a camera that blends upwards, revealing a UI displaying collected voice lines ("Memories") and artifact lore ("Artifacts").

    This menu could be activated by the player, but also automatically appeared when discovering and interacting with an artifact collectable.
