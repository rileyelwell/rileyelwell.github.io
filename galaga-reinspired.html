Galaga Reinspired

Overall Description

Drawing inspiration from the classic Galaga, step into the cockpit of a sleek, 2D space shooter. Command your nimble spacecraft and embark on a relentless mission to blast waves of ever-appearing enemy ships. Navigate through a thrilling array of three distinct ship types, each with its own unique movement mechanics, adding layers of excitement to the challenge. Aim for the stars, as your prowess in the game is directly measured by your high score â€“ more ships, the better!

My Role & The Process

Project Type: Personal, Solo Role: Designer, Programmer, Artist Software: Unity, Aseprite Languages: C#

For my first Unity project without relying on tutorials, I sought to revamp an existing game's elements while infusing it with my creative vision. I stuck with the infinite gameplay loop but streamlined the pattern variety of Galaga. In Aseprite, I meticulously crafted pixel art ships, experimenting with animations on one of the enemy vessels. A significant challenge involved mastering the collision dynamics between bullets, ships, and the camera's outer bounds. Leveraging box colliders, rigidbodies, and code, I successfully recreated the shooting gallery experience reminiscent of Galaga. The most time-intensive endeavor was the development of the Object Pooling system, where I meticulously configured specific Game Objects as "managers" to orchestrate ship and bullet spawning, positioning, and movement. This optimization significantly reduced the number of active Game Objects in the scene, resulting in a substantial approximate 10 percent boost in performance. The finishing touches involved implementing a simplistic UI design and creating a system to monitor the user's current and highest scores.

What I Learned & What I Would Improve

This project has been a valuable learning experience, providing me with insights into the entire game development process as an individual within the Unity engine. I've gained a deeper understanding of various components, how Game Objects operate within a scene, and the mechanics of object spawning. One standout concept that I've embraced is the significance of object pooling, which proved to be a crucial and novel addition to the final product.

Given the chance to enhance this project, I'd begin by implementing a level system similar to the original Galaga game. This would involve designing distinct levels, each featuring unique enemy ship movement patterns, varying ship quantities, and potentially introducing new ships with different mechanics. In addition to this core enhancement, I'd introduce bonus levels amd add animations for all ships and bullets to inject dynamism into the gameplay. Furthermore, I would experiment with a high score leaderboard with multiple slots to encourage friendly competition and record top achievements.
